Alexandria

Alexandria, a port city on Egypt's Mediterranean coast, is known for its historical significance and beautiful coastal views.

Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Qaitbay Citadel

    Qaitbay Citadel is a historic fortress located on the Mediterranean Sea's coast, built on the site of the ancient Lighthouse of Alexandria.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Roman Theater

    The Roman Theater in Alexandria is an ancient amphitheater that dates back to the Roman period, offering a glimpse into the city's historical past.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Graeco-Roman Museum

    The Graeco-Roman Museum showcases artifacts from the Greek and Roman periods of Alexandria’s history.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

Luxor

Luxor is a city on the Nile River in southern Egypt. It is known for its ancient temples and tombs, including the Valley of the Kings.

Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Karnak Temple

    Karnak Temple is a vast complex of temples in Luxor dedicated to the Theban triad of Amun, Mut, and Khonsu.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Luxor Museum

    The Luxor Museum is an archaeological museum in Luxor that showcases artifacts from the tombs of the Valley of the Kings and other ancient Egyptian sites.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Valley of the Kings

    The Valley of the Kings is a burial site in Luxor where many of Egypt's Pharaohs and nobles were buried. It is renowned for its richly decorated tombs.

    Learn more on Wikipedia
