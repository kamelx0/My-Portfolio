Famous Landmarks in Egypt

Explore the most iconic landmarks in Cairo, Alexandria, and Luxor.

Cairo

Cairo, the capital of Egypt, is a bustling metropolis rich in history. It is home to the Giza Pyramids and other historic landmarks.

  • The Pyramids of Giza

    The Pyramids of Giza are one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. They were built as tombs for the Pharaohs and are renowned for their architectural grandeur.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Cairo Citadel

    The Cairo Citadel is a medieval Islamic fortification located on Mokattam Hill. It served as the center of Egypt's government and military for centuries.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Cairo Tower

    The Cairo Tower is a concrete observation tower in Cairo. It offers panoramic views of the city and the Nile River.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

Alexandria

Alexandria, a port city on the Mediterranean Sea, is known for its rich history, including the famous Library of Alexandria.

  • Qaitbay Citadel

    Qaitbay Citadel is a 15th-century defensive fortress located on the Mediterranean Sea coast. It was built on the site of the ancient Lighthouse of Alexandria.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Roman Theater

    The Roman Theater in Alexandria is an ancient amphitheater built during the Roman period. It is one of the few Roman theaters in Egypt.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Graeco-Roman Museum

    The Graeco-Roman Museum in Alexandria showcases artifacts from the Greco-Roman period, including statues, pottery, and coins.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

Luxor

Luxor, often referred to as the world's greatest open-air museum, is renowned for its incredible array of temples and tombs from ancient Egypt.

  • Karnak Temple

    The Karnak Temple is a vast religious complex and one of the largest temples ever constructed in ancient Egypt, dedicated to the gods Amun, Mut, and Khonsu.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Luxor Museum

    The Luxor Museum houses an extensive collection of artifacts from the temples and tombs of Luxor, providing insights into the rich history of the area.

    Learn more on Wikipedia

  • Valley of the Kings

    The Valley of the Kings is a burial site for many of Egypt's New Kingdom Pharaohs. The tombs here are famous for their elaborate decoration and historical significance.

    Learn more on Wikipedia
